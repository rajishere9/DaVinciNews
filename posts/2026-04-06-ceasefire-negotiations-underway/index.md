**ISLAMABAD / WASHINGTON** — While the world watches the clock on President Trump’s Tuesday night ultimatum, a parallel track of intense diplomacy has emerged. Reports early Monday indicate that the United States, Iran, and key regional mediators are actively discussing a comprehensive 45-day ceasefire framework intended to halt the escalating regional war.

### The Two-Phase Framework
The proposed ceasefire, first reported by Axios, is structured around a two-phase implementation plan. The initial phase would involve an immediate cessation of major aerial and maritime hostilities, including a temporary pause on strikes against critical infrastructure. This would provide a vital window for humanitarian relief and the de-escalation of maritime tensions in the Strait of Hormuz.

The second phase aims to facilitate broader negotiations toward a permanent end to the conflict, which has claimed thousands of lives across Iran, Lebanon, and Israel since late February.

### A Tense Backdrop
The diplomatic push comes at a moment of maximum pressure. President Trump has maintained his "Tuesday, 8:00 P.M. Eastern Time" deadline for the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, threatening severe strikes on Iranian power plants and bridges if the demand is not met.

Over the last 24 hours, the physical conflict has remained intense:
- **Airstrikes:** Coordinated strikes in Iran have reportedly resulted in over 25 deaths, hitting residential areas in Haifa, Eslamshahr, and Qom.
- **Retaliation:** Iran has continued its missile campaign against Israel and Gulf neighbors, with strikes reported in Haifa and injuries in the UAE following interceptions.

### Regional Stakes
Regional mediators, including Pakistan and Qatar, are working against an increasingly volatile clock. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi stated that while Tehran is determined to defend its sovereignty, it is seeking a "conclusive and lasting" end to the conflict.

However, the path to an agreement is fraught with obstacles. Tehran continues to demand compensation for war damages as a prerequisite for reopening the Strait, while the Trump administration remains committed to its strategy of "maximum pressure" until its core strategic objectives—specifically the neutralization of Iran's drone and missile capabilities—are verified as complete.

### Global Market Reaction
The news of potential talks has brought a slight reprieve to global markets, though uncertainty remains high. Brent crude is currently hovering near $110 a barrel, reflecting the market’s cautious skepticism that a diplomatic breakthrough can be achieved before the Tuesday deadline.

As mediators race to finalize the framework, the coming 36 hours will likely determine whether the region moves toward a fragile peace or descends into an even more destructive phase of total war.
