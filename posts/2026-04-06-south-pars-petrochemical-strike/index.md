**SOUTH PARS / TEHRAN** — The industrial war between Israel and Iran escalated further on Monday as coordinated Israeli airstrikes targeted critical petrochemical facilities in the South Pars natural gas field. The strikes, which reportedly killed a senior Revolutionary Guard intelligence commander and at least 25 others, mark a significant expansion of the air campaign against Iran's energy infrastructure just hours ahead of a U.S.-imposed deadline.

### Targeting the Economic Lifeline
The South Pars field, shared between Iran and Qatar, is the world's largest natural gas field and the backbone of Iran's energy economy. Reports from the region indicate multiple direct hits on processing units, resulting in massive fires and the suspension of operations at several key facilities. 

Among the high-profile casualties is Maj. Gen. Majid Khademi, the head of intelligence for the IRGC, who was reportedly killed in a related strike in Tehran. The Iranian Revolutionary Guard has vowed "crushing retaliation" against both Israeli and American interests in the region.

### The Looming Deadline
The strikes occurred against the backdrop of a looming deadline set by U.S. President Donald Trump, who has demanded that Iran reopen the Strait of Hormuz by Tuesday or face "hellish" strikes on its broader infrastructure, including power plants and bridges. 

Despite the military pressure, Tehran has officially rejected a new 45-day ceasefire proposal floated by Egyptian and Pakistani mediators. A spokesperson for the Iranian government stated that Iran will not accept a "temporary pause" while its sovereign territory remains under attack and its economy remains crippled by the blockade.

### Regional Retaliation
In the hours following the South Pars strikes, Iran launched a fresh wave of missile and drone attacks:
- **Haifa, Israel:** Multiple missiles struck residential areas and an industrial factory, resulting in several injuries and significant property damage.
- **Gulf Neighbors:** A missile interception in the UAE resulted in at least one injury from falling debris, while Saudi air defenses reported engaging multiple incoming drones over the Eastern Province.

### Global Market and Humanitarian Impact
The continued targeting of energy infrastructure has pushed Brent crude near $110 a barrel. Beyond the economic toll, humanitarian organizations are sounding the alarm over a critical shortage of medical supplies and food in the region. Many essential shipments, including life-saving medication for Yemen, remain stuck in Dubai ports due to the restrictions in the Strait of Hormuz.

As the Tuesday deadline approaches, the prospect of a diplomatic breakthrough remains dim, with both sides intensifying their physical and rhetorical maneuvers in what has become a grueling war of attrition.
