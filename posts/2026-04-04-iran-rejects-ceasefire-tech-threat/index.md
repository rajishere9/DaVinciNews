**TEHRAN / DUBAI** — The hope for a temporary pause in the rapidly expanding Middle East conflict vanished on Saturday morning as Iran officially rejected a U.S.-led proposal for a 48-hour ceasefire. The rejection came alongside a direct threat against 18 American technology firms, including Oracle, as the war shifts from the physical battlefield to global digital infrastructure.

### The Rejected Ceasefire
According to reports from Iran’s semi-official Fars News Agency, the Islamic Republic has dismissed a 48-hour ceasefire proposal delivered via a third-party mediator. The proposal, intended to provide a window for humanitarian relief and the potential recovery of downed aircraft pilots, was labeled "insincere" by Tehran officials.

U.S. President Donald Trump, speaking earlier on the conflict, maintained a defiant stance, stating that the recent downing of American military jets would not derail negotiations, though he acknowledged the gravity of the situation. "No, not at all. No, it's war. We're in war," Trump told reporters.

### Digital Warfare: Targeting the Tech Giants
In a significant escalation of rhetoric, the Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) has issued a "black list" of 18 U.S. companies accused of "terrorist espionage" against the Iranian state. Oracle was specifically named as a primary target.

The threat was underscored by physical events in Dubai, where authorities reported that debris from intercepted drones damaged the facade of a building housing Oracle's regional offices. While no injuries were reported in that specific incident, the IRGC indicated that digital and physical assets of these firms are now considered legitimate military targets.

### Expanding Fronts: From Hormuz to Bab al-Mandeb
While the world has focused on the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, Iranian Parliament Speaker Mohammad-Bagher Ghalibaf signaled that the conflict could soon engulf the Bab al-Mandeb Strait as well. In a post on X, Ghalibaf questioned the share of global oil, LNG, and fertilizer shipments transiting the waterway, a move seen by analysts as a clear warning to commercial shipping in the Red Sea.

### Regional Casualties and Displacement
The human cost of the conflict continues to climb. Over the last 24 hours:
- **Lebanon:** Israeli airstrikes targeted "militant infrastructure" in Beirut and towns in eastern Lebanon. More than 1,300 people have been killed in recent strikes, and approximately one-fifth of Lebanon's population is now displaced.
- **Israel:** Iranian missile strikes and cluster munitions were reported over central Israel, including Tel Aviv, causing residential damage and power outages.
- **Iraq:** A drone strike hit oil storage facilities west of Basra, raising fresh alarms over the safety of international energy infrastructure.

### Virtual Lockdown in Tehran
Domestically, the Iranian Ministry of Science and Technology has announced that all university courses nationwide will move online until further notice. This move is interpreted as a measure to protect students while maintaining social control as the country remains under constant threat of aerial bombardment.

As the 48-hour ceasefire window closes without agreement, the region braces for what military analysts describe as a "total war" phase, with no clear path back to the negotiating table.
