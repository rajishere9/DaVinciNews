**NEW YORK / TEHRAN** — Geopolitical gridlock reached a critical peak on Tuesday as Russia and China jointly vetoed a United Nations Security Council resolution aimed at reopening the Strait of Hormuz. The veto came just hours before U.S. President Donald Trump’s 8:00 P.M. Eastern Time deadline, effectively ending the final international diplomatic effort to prevent a massive escalation of the conflict.

### The UN Standoff
The resolution, which had been significantly watered down to gain broader support, sought to authorize \"all necessary measures\" to ensure the freedom of navigation through the world's most critical oil chokepoint. While the U.S. and its allies argued the resolution was essential for global energy stability, Moscow and Beijing characterized it as a \"pretext for further illegal aggression\" against Iranian sovereignty.

The veto has left the international community with no collective mechanism to address the blockade, passing the initiative back to individual state actors just as the clock enters its final hour.

### \"A Whole Civilization Will Die Tonight\"
In Washington, the rhetoric has reached an apocalyptic tone. President Trump reiterated his warning on Tuesday, stating that \"a whole civilization will die tonight\" if Iran does not yield. The President has threatened \"complete demolition\" of Iran’s infrastructure, including bridges and power plants, should the 8:00 P.M. ET hour pass without a deal.

Military analysts report that U.S. and Israeli forces have already expanded their strike lists, with over 50 military targets on Iran's Kharg Island reportedly hit in the last 24 hours.

### Iran Prepares for Total War
Tehran has responded with a mobilization of its own. In addition to rejecting the latest ceasefire proposals, Iranian officials have called on the nation’s youth to form \"human chains\" around critical utilities to act as human shields against impending airstrikes. 

Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian remained defiant, stating that 14 million Iranians are prepared to sacrifice their lives to defend the nation. Meanwhile, the country’s nationwide internet blackout has entered its 39th day, effectively isolating the Iranian public from global communications as the deadline nears.

### Humanitarian and Religious Condemnation
The escalating threats have drawn sharp criticism from global leaders. UN Human Rights Chief Volker Türk described the threat to annihilate an entire civilization as \"sickening,\" while Pope Leo XIV stated that the targeting of civilian infrastructure is \"truly unacceptable\" and a violation of international law.

### Market Instability
As the deadline approaches:
- **Oil Volatility:** Brent crude remains highly unsettled, hovering near $110 a barrel as markets price in the likelihood of a total regional conflict.
- **Regional Defense:** Saudi Arabia has reportedly closed its main bridge to Bahrain due to Iranian threats, while air defenses across the Gulf remain on the highest state of alert.

With no diplomatic path remaining through the United Nations, the world now waits to see if the 8:00 P.M. ET deadline will mark the start of the most destructive phase of the war to date.
